Why Solar is a Better Investment Than Buying Bonds

Solar power is a better investment than buying bonds. Solar power has seen a steady increase in popularity over the past few years, and for good reason: it’s not just environmentally friendly, but also financially wise. The cost of solar panels continues to drop as their efficiency rises, and the government offers tax credits (30% on residential systems) and grants (up to $2,000 on commercial projects). If you decide that your home or business would benefit from going solar at this point then you should know what your options are before investing any money into the process.

You’ll need to find an installer who will come out and perform an assessment of your property – they’ll need to determine how much space is available for panels, where the sun shines most strongly, and whether or not your roof is suited for solar. You can usually find a list of qualified installers through your state’s solar energy authority (see Resources below).

After the assessment is complete, you’ll have a few different options for how to go about purchasing and installing your solar panels. You can buy the panels outright, lease them from the installer, or take out a loan to finance the purchase. Each option has its own set of pros and cons, so it’s important to do your research before making a decision.
